Q&A Preparation reference document
Estimation & CPM
Why not use analogous estimation?
No prior C project to draw from — expert judgement was our only option. Bottom-up decomposition via WBS gave us the granularity.

Why did estimates fail?
Planning fallacy: we estimated best-case as base-case. CHAOS data shows this is systemic — 66% failure rate. PERT corrects by forcing three-point thinking.

How does your app handle cycles?
Parser rejects circular dependencies with an error — CPM requires a DAG. We validate adjacency before forward pass.
Technical Decisions
Why C over Python?
Iron Triangle trade-off: we chose learning depth over delivery speed. If deadline was tighter, Python would have been correct.

Would you choose C again?
No. We’d spike the language risk first — 30 minutes of prototype would have surfaced the malloc difficulty before committing.
Teamwork & Collaboration
How did you handle the C conflict?
Tuckman Storming: 15-min rule triggered round-robin. McClelland’s affiliation need was met (prior trust), so we could disagree safely. Outcome: stick with C + pair-programming support.

What would you do differently in a new team?
Invest more in Tuckman’s Forming stage — explicit norm-setting. Our shortcuts worked because of prior history; a new team needs that scaffolding built deliberately.

Was Samuel’s struggle a leadership failure?
Partly. Jamie’s implicit PO authority (ADKAR gap) delayed pushback. Servant leadership — leading by supporting — should have surfaced the blocker earlier.
Reflection & Theory
Why Schön over Kolb?
Schön captures reframing mid-action — Samuel’s realisation happened during the sprint, not after. Kolb suits post-hoc cycles.

How does Wilemon & Thamhain apply?
Cole’s phantom bug = ‘Communication Problems’ barrier specifically — miscommunication through artefacts, not words. The fix: automated verification alongside visual output.
