Assessment Outline
During Sprint Week (Figure 1), your group will develop a digital application in ONE of the
following areas:
• Option 1: Automate project cost estimation
• Option 2: Automate critical path identification
The goal is to demonstrate your ability to apply project management and digital technology skills,
collaborate effectively as a team, and reflect critically on your learning and team dynamics.
At the end of the week, your group will:
• Present your developed application to your peers and a panel.
• Reflect on team dynamics and development, including how your group collaborated,
overcame challenges, and responded to feedback.
This is a summative assessment worth 60% of your final grade.
Guidance on getting started:
• Artefact Development Process (Sprint days 1 & 2, Figure 1)
o Choose ONE of the two options (cost estimation or critical path).
o Develop a working prototype or functional demonstration of your application
using either a preferred programming language or formulas/macros in Excel.
o Ensure the application reflects sound project management principles and is
practical and functional.
o Keep a record of your development process (tools used, decisions made,
challenges faced).
NOTE: You are not assessed on the technical complexity of the artefact you are developing, but on
your understanding and application of project management concepts.
• Group Reflection (end of Sprint Day 2, Figure 1)
Your reflection should focus on your experience developing Option A/B, with particular attention
to team dynamics and your development as a team. Consider the following prompts:
o How did your team organise and assign roles?
o How did you handle challenges or conflicts?

o How did the team make decisions and solve problems?
o How did you respond to feedback from peers or the panel?
o How did individual contributions support team success?
o What did you learn about collaboration, leadership, and project management?

Presentation Instructions
When: The presentations will be scheduled during days 3 & 4 of the Sprint week (Thursday and
Friday)
Duration: 10 minutes (presentation) + 5 minutes (Q&A)
Structure:
1. Demonstration of your application
o You will demonstrate your application live during the presentation
o Slides must include ONE of the following:
▪ A link to your prototype, or
▪ A link to the Excel file (if applicable), or
▪ A user-manual-style demonstration of your application, including
screenshots and step-by-step instructions, so the panel can understand its
functionality.
2. Discussion of your development process
o You will present the key decisions your team made during the development of the
artefact on Sprint Days 1 and 2.
3. Team reflection on team dynamics and learning
o Please ensure you provide a PPT slide that outlines the key points you are
discussing
o You may present your reflection verbally during your presentation, but the panel
may ask follow-up questions, so ensure all members are familiar with the key
points.
All group members must participate in the presentation.

Submission Instructions
Each group must submit their presentation slides via the QM+ submission link.
• The slides must be the same as those used in the presentation.
o Your slides must include a demonstration of the artefact, a discussion of the
development process, and a reflection on team dynamics and lessons learned,

• Failure to submit the slides via QM+ will be treated as a non-submission, and the
group will receive 0 marks for this component.
Professional Presentation, Academic Integrity,
Confidentiality, and Compliance
Your slides should be clearly organised and professionally presented. Write in the first person
where appropriate for reflection, but maintain a high standard of grammar, clarity, and structure.
All academic sources used should be cited using the Harvard referencing style.
For support with reflection and academic writing, please refer to the learning resources on QM+
or contact the module organiser.
You must not include any confidential or personally identifiable information in your work.
Pseudonymise individuals or organisations where necessary. Plagiarism, falsification, or
inappropriate use of AI tools will be treated as academic misconduct. Ensure you are familiar with
the types of academic misconduct outlined in the QMUL Academic Integrity and Misconduct
policy available here. If you are unsure about anything related to academic integrity or
misconduct, please reach out to your module organiser.
Generative AI
Use of Generative AI (e.g., Microsoft Copilot or ChatGPT) is permitted for this assessment but must
not be used to generate text which you then submit as your own as this is academic misconduct.
If using spelling or grammar assistive tools, avoid using any Generative AI functionality which
rewrites non-trivial portions of your work.
Avoid relying on Generative AI (e.g., ChatGPT) to format your references as these tools can often
produce references which do not exist.
For more information, please see https://www.qmul.ac.uk/library/academic-skills/studentguide-to-generative-ai/.

Late Submissions and Extenuating Circumstances
Assessments submitted after the published deadline will be marked as late and will receive a
penalty, unless you have approved Extenuating Circumstances (EC).
You may submit up to seven calendar days after the deadline but a penalty of 5% of the total
marks available will be applied to the assessment for each 24-hour period (or part of it) that the
work is late (e.g. five marks deducted each day from a total of 100). After seven calendar days
the assessment will be recorded as a non-submission and given a mark of zero.
For more information, you can find the latest Assessment Handbook and Extenuating
Circumstances policy via the QMUL policies page here: https://www.qmul.ac.uk/governanceand-legal-services/policy/policies-by-category/
