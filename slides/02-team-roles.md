## Speaker Notes

We allocated by strengths — I took Scrum Master knowing the team, Jamie owned the algorithm from his CPM research, Cole brought RayLib experience, Hamza was strongest at spotting cross-module issues. It worked because our 2-day timeline made speed essential, but nobody stretches. Longer projects need rotation for growth.

I facilitated rather than directed — all four were self-motivated, so directive leadership would have undermined ownership. We formalised accountability with a RACI matrix: each WBS package had one person Responsible and Accountable, with Integration having three Consulted because cross-module risk sat there.

When Samuel's parser took 3× estimate, Cole absorbed some testing scope Day 2 — keeping workload equitable. And once we spotted our missing Completer Finisher, Hamza shifted to running full integration rather than just type checks.
