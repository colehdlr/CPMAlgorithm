## Speaker Script

"To summarise: we built a CPM application in C that correctly performs forward and backward passes, calculates float, identifies the critical path, and visualises it as an interactive AON network diagram — demonstrating the scheduling theory from Kelley and Walker (1959) that we studied in Weeks 4 and 5. We validated it against the brief's test scenario and cross-checked with an independent Python implementation. We delivered within our Iron Triangle constraints: a two-day timeline, a four-person team, and a working CPM application with visualisation. More importantly, we learned that building a project management tool taught us project management. This module asks us to reflect on how we plan, estimate, and lead. Our application automates the scheduling theory; our team process applied the leadership and communication practices from Weeks 2 and 3. The tool and the process are the same discipline. Tuckman's final stage — Adjourning — involves reflection and closure. This presentation is our adjourning: capturing learning, recognising contributions, and communicating outcomes. As Pinto and Kharbanda (1996) identify, postmortems and reflection are critical success factors — this presentation ensures that lessons are captured rather than lost. Through this project we have demonstrated K15 in estimation and scheduling, S5 and S6 in methodology application and project management, K8 and K9 in teamwork and leadership, and B1 and B2 in professional collaboration. Thank you. We welcome your questions."
