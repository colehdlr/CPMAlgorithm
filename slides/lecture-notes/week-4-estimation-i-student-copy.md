## Page 7

Project Estimation
• Involves forecasting the resources, time, and costs required to 
complete a project. 
• Important for monitoring and control
• Methods of estimation:
• Expert judgement: based on past experience or the expertise of other team 
members. 
• Three-point estimation: using a weighted average that considers the best-case, 
worst-case and most likely durations. 
• Analogous estimation: drawn on data from historical similar projects.
• Parametric estimation: statistical relationships are used to form a relationship 
between historical data and project variables to forecast.
• Bottom-up estimation: Breakdown the project into the smallest, most 
manageable components (packages) and each component is individually 
estimated.
• Top-down estimation: draw on existing knowledge, industry benchmarks, or 
expert opinion to establish a project timeframe and budget.

Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
• Project Breakdown Structure Vs Work 
Breakdown Structure
• Project Breakdown Structure: a 
hierarchical structure of things that the 
project will make or outcomes that it will 
deliver. It can be thought of as the project 
“shopping list.”
• Work Breakdown structure: a hierarchical 
structure of project activity. If you like it 
represents the project “to do list.” Its focus 
is on “work” not “things.” 
• Both are helpful for monitoring and control: 
helps to avoid scope creep, budget and 
schedule overruns
• Sets the foundation for creating a Gantt chart. 
• Work Breakdown Structure VS Project 
Breakdown Structure
(Institute of Project Management, 2022)

## Page 14

Types of WBS
•  Deliverable-based: Focus on the deliverables of 
the project (Individual services, key groups of 
deliverables and products delivered via the project)
• Phase-based: Focus on project phases and related 
activities (planning, design, development, testing, 
closeout)
•  Resource-based: Focus on the resources of the 
project (staffing, equipment, software used in the 
project)
•  Responsibility-based: Focus on organising based 
on the teams and units that work on the project.
•  Risk-based: organised by types risks encountered 
in the project (people, environment and equipment 
related)
